The "Machine Learning" course offers a comprehensive look at fundamental concepts and methodologies. You will explore the core paradigm of ML, covering supervised learning (Classification and Regression) and unsupervised learning (Clustering and Dimensionality Reduction).   

Key supervised methods covered include Linear Regression, Decision Trees, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Generalized Linear Models (GLM), and Support Vector Machines (SVM). For unsupervised methods, the course details Hierarchical Clustering and K-means, alongside the dimensionality reduction technique, Principal Component Analysis (PCA).   

Emphasis is placed on practical aspects, such as the importance of feature engineering and choosing appropriate distance metrics like the Minkowski metric (Manhattan and Euclidean distances). Finally, the course reviews essential model evaluation techniques, including performance metrics (Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F-beta, ROC/AUC) and validation strategies like the Hold-out set and Cross-validation.
